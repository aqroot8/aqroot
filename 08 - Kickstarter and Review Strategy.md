# Kickstarter and Review Strategy

## Prototype-to-final strategy
Demo a functional dev-board prototype proving core features work (NFC, sub-GHz, IR, LoRa,
UI). Finish custom PCB, certified antenna tuning, and final enclosure during the funded
production phase. Standard practice for hardware campaigns (Kode Dot did this too).

Rule: never demo a feature that doesn't actually work yet on camera or in campaign
materials. Label anything unfinished as "in development."

## Sending prototypes to YouTubers
- Set a clear embargo date so reviews go live at or after campaign launch.
- Be upfront with reviewers about what's dev-board-wired vs final design — this lands
  much better with audiences than being caught out later.
- Prepare a short press kit: spec sheet, competitive positioning vs Flipper Zero/Kode Dot,
  a few good product photos.
- Budget for 2-4 prototype units if seeding multiple reviewers around the same time
  (~$65-130 each at Stage 1 dev-board cost — see BOM and Cost Tracker).
- Check the SX1262 module's certification conditions before shipping units externally —
  modifying the RF section can void FCC/CE certification.
